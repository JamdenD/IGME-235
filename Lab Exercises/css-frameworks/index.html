Deep Rock Galactic

My Dad loves first person shooters and would frequently hand me his controller and say “hey play this game for me while I make dinner” and I would immediately panic. First person shooters always stress me out, so obviously for this assignment a co-op first person shooter was the go to.

My Dad loves Deep Rock and has been talking it up to me for years now, so I borrowed my Dad’s copy and committed myself and… I was so incredibly pleasantly surprised! As someone who has committed myself to being incredibly bad at FPS, I had so much fun playing this game. The environments are beautiful (something I love in games)(also procedurally generated??? awesome) and the music was fantastic (also something I love)(like so good I started listening to it on its own) but the best part about it was how incredibly unserious it was. Something about getting my dwarf a silly mustache and having him get sturdy next to a jukebox in a little hospital dressing gown after I fail a mission takes away the sting of not being very good at this type of game.

The gameplay itself was also more enjoyable than I thought it’d be. I think since shooting isn’t the only goal (you also have to mine things) there is a good balance between the two and I found it way less intimidating as someone who isn’t super experienced with first person shooters. I’m gonna keep playing it.

PEAK

PEAK is a lovely game where you and your friend(s) try to climb a mountain. I went into this game not knowing anything about the controls or really how to play the game, just that it was popular and a fun co-op game, and it pleasantly surprised me. One thing I will say, although there is no tutorial or anything, the onboarding for this game is fantastic because it relies on player curiosity to teach them about the environment and controls rather than simply telling them outright. This was really refreshing because I expected to have to go through a super annoying tutorial level or something when I first started, but you’re kind of dropped right into it.

Once I got my bearings after a bit of a pathetic first run, I realized even more how smart this game is at hooking players and their desire to learn, explore, and puzzle things out. Don’t know what eating that mushroom does? Eat it! It might heal you, it might poison you and make your screen look super silly for like 30 seconds. This game both punishes and rewards curiosity in the best way, and even when you are punished it feels fun and rewarding in a way because you just learned more about your environment, almost in true scout fashion (which makes sense because the player characters are scouts).

A super cute and fun game and I will be making more and more people play it with me. I am addicted.

Project Zomboid

So this game was incredibly frustrating. I didn’t rage quit per say, but I quit just before the point of rage. I tried. I even went back to the game the night after I initially tried it to try and pinpoint why I disliked it so much.

Lemme start with the tutorial. It literally kills you on purpose. Which is like fine whatever it’s a game about zombies and you’re poking fun at the player. The controls were a little bit confusing, especially because of the perspective that you are viewing the world from, and the menu navigation just makes things all the more frustrating because it covers up literally everything while the whole point of the game is to 1) scavenge stuff and 2) stay on your toes, so it seems counter-intuitive.

Anyway, I started a new real game. There were 4 game modes, I chose the second easiest one, aka one step above creative mode. It was called “Builder” and it said it was for a more “laid-back experience”. I was not used to the controls and I didn’t want to be stressed and this seemed like a base-management game so I picked that. I literally died within 5 minutes and had to start a whole new game because I got completely overrun with zombies. I figured “oh… that was probably a fluke…” and started a new game and the SAME THING HAPPENED. I don’t know, it was just frustrating.

I thought the character creation in this game was really cool since it was sort of like DnD classes but way more extensive and like real life occupations and how they could help you in any sort of way during a zombie apocalypse. Yknow what would make me like character creation a whole lot more? IF I DIDN’T DIE AND HAVE TO MAKE A WHOLE NEW CHARACTER 3 MINUTES LATER. I didn’t have fun.

Root

I am not any kind of expert at strategy board games like this and my family doesn’t play a lot of board games so I thought this would be a good game to review. The first thing I have to point out is that I did play this game with two people, which is gonna poison this review a lot.

I actually really enjoyed the strategical intricacies of this game and the fact that the play experience could be completely different from one game to the next. The board is semi-randomized for the game, which I quite liked, and of course you can play as any one of the four (or in my case 8 since my boyfriend has 2 expansions for the game) different factions, who all have different goals and actions that they can do on their turn.

I think playing this game 1-on-1 for the first time did this game a major disservice. We both had to play very aggressive factions to actually have fun (according to the rules, we didn’t just do that) and that just was kind of really depressing when I didn’t really know how my faction or his faction worked and he knew how both worked and what to look out for and stop early whereas I was just kind of going with the flow. My boyfriend played the mole faction and I think those were a little unbalanced, especially with a specific card he had, and he kept killing my little cat guys and it made me so sad :(. I think I was just a little unlucky with how everything was laid out and the fact that it was my first time playing the game. I definitely want to play it again and with more people and learn more about how the game and the different factions work together because it seems like there are a lot of really cool systems in this game.

Thank you for reading!
